Sprint 5

Reflection of Foundation's Core Skills

What impact has the core learning had on my Foundations learning journey?

The core learning has helped me look internally and find some of the reasons why I behave and think in certain ways. Because I know a little bit more about why I am the way I am, I am empowered to change.

What have I learned about myself as a result of the core learning?

I have learned that I have a fixed mindset for some things and a growth mindset for others. Learning about the two mindsets gave me an insight into how I can reach my full potential. Introducing myself to my peers really highlighted how shy I am to share personal information with anyone especially strangers. I always thought of myself as an open book but I now realise that I am only an open book to my very close family. Mostly the core learning taught me about myself, and broke down a lot of misconceptions I had held.

What surprised me about the core learning?

In some ways I am resistant to change, especially when it comes to things emotional because I have of emotional baggage. The core learning was focussed around us and we needed to go out and find what we could. Because of this style of learning I was more receptive and gave myself more freedom to think that I can change by my own volition. This was the most surprising part for me because I was expecting myself to disregard or disagree with the material for fear of change.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

Definitely the meditation from Sprint 3. Because of past trauma, trying to meditate or practice mindfulness in a completely serene quiet environment is very difficult. When my mind goes clear, other things come up that are usually repressed. I have been trying to practice mindfulness while doing something else, such as cooking, embroidery or spending quiet time where I feel most safe - beside my partner.
